{% trans "What is an Amortization Schedule?" %}

{% blocktrans %}An amortization schedule shows how each payment is split between principal and interest over the life of the loan. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal. This calculator generates a full schedule and lets you add optional extra monthly payments to see how much interest and time you can save.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Monthly Payment = P × [r(1+r)^n] ÷ [(1+r)^n − 1]" %}

{% trans "P = principal, r = monthly rate, n = number of payments" %}

{% trans "Interest for each period = balance × monthly rate. Principal = payment − interest. New balance = previous balance − principal." %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "Principal" %}

{% trans "The amount you borrowed. Each payment reduces the principal until the balance is zero." %}

{% trans "Interest" %}

{% trans "The cost of borrowing. Early in the loan, most of each payment is interest; over time, more goes to principal." %}

{% trans "Extra payments" %}

{% trans "Any amount paid beyond the required payment goes directly to principal, reducing the balance faster and saving interest." %}
