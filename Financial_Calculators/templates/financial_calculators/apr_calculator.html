{% trans "What is APR?" %}

{% blocktrans %}The interest rate is the cost of borrowing the principal. APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the interest rate plus fees (origination, discount points, and other closing costs), giving you the true yearly cost of the loan. APR is always equal to or higher than the stated rate. Lenders are required to disclose APR so you can compare offers.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "APR reflects: (Interest + Fees) over the life of the loan, expressed as an annual rate" %}

{% trans "Net loan = Loan amount − Total fees. Payment is based on loan amount; APR is solved so that payments match the net proceeds." %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "Stated rate vs APR" %}

{% trans "The stated (nominal) rate is the interest rate on the loan. APR includes that rate plus upfront fees, so it represents the true cost of borrowing." %}

{% trans "Origination and fees" %}

{% trans "Origination fee is a one-time charge (often a percentage of the loan). Discount points (1 point = 1% of loan) buy a lower rate. Other fees can include processing or closing costs." %}

{% trans "Why compare APR?" %}

{% trans "A loan with a lower interest rate but high fees can have a higher APR. Always compare APR when shopping; consider total cost and how long you will keep the loan." %}
