{% trans "Semi-Annual Compounding" %}

{% trans "Unlike US mortgages which often compound monthly, Canadian mortgages typically compound semi-annually (twice a year). This means the 'Effective Annual Rate' (EAR) is slightly lower than if it were compounded monthly, saving you money on interest." %}

{% trans "Term vs. Amortization" %}

{% trans "The 'Amortization' is the total life of your loan (e.g., 25 years). The 'Term' is how long your rate is locked in (e.g., 5 years). You will renew your mortgage multiple times over its life, potentially at different rates." %}
