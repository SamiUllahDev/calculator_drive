{% trans "What is a CD (Certificate of Deposit)?" %}

{% blocktrans %}A certificate of deposit (CD) is a savings product offered by banks and credit unions with a fixed interest rate and a fixed term. You agree to leave your money deposited for the term length—from a few months to several years—in exchange for a typically higher rate than regular savings accounts. At maturity, you receive your initial deposit plus the accrued interest.{% endblocktrans %}

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

{% trans "P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compoundings per year, t = years" %}

{% blocktrans %}This calculator shows how much your CD will be worth at maturity, how much interest you earn, and how your balance grows over time. You can compare different term lengths and compounding frequencies.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "APY (Annual Percentage Yield)" %}

{% trans "The annual rate of return including compound interest. APY is what banks typically advertise and is higher than the nominal rate when interest compounds more than once per year." %}

{% trans "Compounding Frequency" %}

{% trans "How often interest is added to your balance—daily, monthly, quarterly, semi-annually, or annually. More frequent compounding generally means slightly higher effective returns." %}

{% trans "Term" %}

{% trans "The length of time you commit to leave your money in the CD. Longer terms often offer higher APYs but your funds are locked until maturity (or you pay an early-withdrawal penalty)." %}

{% trans "Understanding Your Results" %}

{% trans "Final Amount" %}

{% trans "The total value of your CD at maturity: your initial deposit plus all interest earned." %}

{% trans "Interest Earned" %}

{% trans "The difference between the final amount and your initial deposit. This is the earnings from the CD over the term." %}

{% trans "Effective APY" %}

{% trans "The annualized return after accounting for compounding. When compounding is more frequent than annual, effective APY is slightly higher than the stated rate." %}

{% trans "Deposit vs Interest Chart" %}

{% trans "A doughnut chart showing how much of your final balance is your original deposit (principal) vs. interest earned." %}

{% trans "Balance Growth Chart" %}

{% trans "A line chart showing how your balance grows month by month (or at sampled points) over the term." %}
