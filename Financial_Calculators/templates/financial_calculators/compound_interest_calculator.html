{% trans "Understanding Compound Interest" %}

{% trans "The Power of Compounding" %}

{% trans "Compound interest is often called the eighth wonder of the world. Unlike simple interest, compound interest earns interest on your interest, leading to exponential growth over time." %}

{% trans "Compound Frequency Matters" %}

{% trans "The more frequently interest compounds, the more you earn. Daily compounding earns more than monthly, which earns more than annually. However, the differences are usually small." %}

{% trans "Time Is Your Greatest Asset" %}

{% trans "The longer your money compounds, the more dramatic the growth. Starting early, even with smaller amounts, often beats starting later with larger amounts." %}

{% trans "The Rule of 72" %}

{% trans "Divide 72 by your interest rate to estimate how many years it takes to double your money. At 7% interest, your money doubles approximately every 10 years." %}

{% trans "Frequently Asked Questions" %}

{% trans "Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal. Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any accumulated interest, leading to faster growth over time." %}

{% trans "More frequent compounding means your interest starts earning interest sooner, resulting in higher total returns. However, the difference between daily and monthly compounding is often minimal." %}

{% trans "Savings accounts typically offer 0.5-5%. Bonds may return 2-6%. Stock market has historically returned around 7-10% annually (inflation-adjusted). Use rates appropriate for your investment type." %}

{% trans "Contributing at the beginning of each period results in slightly higher returns because your money has more time to compound. This is often called 'annuity due' vs 'ordinary annuity'." %}
