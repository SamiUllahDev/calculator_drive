{% trans "Complete User Guide" %}

{% trans "What is the Debt Consolidation Calculator?" %}

{% trans "This calculator compares your current debts (multiple loans or cards) with a single consolidation loan. You enter each debt's balance, interest rate, and monthly payment; then you enter the proposed consolidation loan's rate, term, and any fees." %}

{% trans "Results show whether consolidation saves money (monthly and total interest), your new single payment, a recommendation, an interest comparison chart, and a sample amortization schedule for the consolidation loan." %}

{% trans "Key Metrics" %}

{% trans "Monthly Savings" %}

{% trans "Current total monthly payment minus the consolidation loan payment. Positive means a lower payment." %}

{% trans "Interest Savings" %}

{% trans "Total interest you would pay on current debts minus total interest on the consolidation loan. Positive means you save on interest." %}

{% trans "Recommendation" %}

{% trans "Based on your inputs: beneficial (saves money and time), saves interest but higher payment, lowers payment but more interest, or not beneficial." %}

{% trans "How to Use This Calculator" %}

  1. {% trans "Add each current debt: name, balance, interest rate, and monthly payment." %}
  2. {% trans "Enter the consolidation loan's interest rate, term (years), and any origination fees." %}
  3. {% trans "Click Calculate to see monthly savings, interest savings, recommendation, and comparison chart." %}
  4. {% trans "Use Remove to delete a debt row; Add Debt to add more." %}

{% trans "Understanding Your Results" %}

{% trans "Time to payoff for current debts is estimated using your entered monthly payments. The consolidation loan amount is the sum of all debt balances plus any fees. If the consolidation rate is lower than your weighted average rate and the term is reasonable, you often save both monthly and total interest." %}

{% trans "If you have upfront fees, consider how many months of monthly savings it takes to break even. The schedule shows principal and interest for the consolidation loan over time." %}

{% trans "Understanding the Chart" %}

{% trans "The bar chart compares current debts vs consolidated: principal (amount borrowed) and interest (cost of borrowing). Lower interest on the consolidated side means you save money." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "Results are estimates. Actual consolidation offers depend on your credit and lender. Don't close paid-off accounts until the new loan is funded." %}
  • {% trans "Including fees in the loan increases the amount you borrow and total interest; compare with and without fees." %}
  • {% trans "Consolidation works best when the new rate is lower than your weighted average and you avoid taking on new debt." %}

{% trans "Tips" %}

•{% trans "Get quotes from multiple lenders (banks, credit unions, online) before consolidating." %}
•{% trans "Check if the consolidation loan has a lower APR than your current weighted average rate." %}
•{% trans "Factor in origination or balance-transfer fees; they can reduce net savings." %}
•{% trans "Use a shorter term if you can afford it to save more interest." %}
