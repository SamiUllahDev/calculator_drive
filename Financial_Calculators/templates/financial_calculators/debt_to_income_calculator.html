{% trans "What is Debt-to-Income Ratio?" %}

{% blocktrans %}Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward paying monthly debts. Lenders use it to assess your ability to manage mortgage payments. A lower DTI generally improves your chances of qualifying for a loan and may help you get better rates.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Front-End DTI = Housing Costs ÷ Gross Income" %}

{% trans "Back-End DTI = Total Monthly Debts ÷ Gross Income" %}

{% blocktrans %}This calculator shows both front-end (housing only) and back-end (all debts) ratios, compares you to common loan program limits, and estimates how much room you have for additional debt.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "What Your Results Mean" %}

{% trans "Front-End DTI (Housing Ratio)" %}

{% trans "Housing costs (PITI) ÷ gross monthly income. Lenders often prefer 28% or less." %}

{% trans "Back-End DTI (Total Debt Ratio)" %}

{% trans "All monthly debt payments ÷ gross income. The qualified mortgage (QM) limit is typically 43%." %}
