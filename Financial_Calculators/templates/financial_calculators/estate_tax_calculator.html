{% trans "What is the Federal Estate Tax?" %}

{% blocktrans %}The federal estate tax is a tax on the transfer of your estate at death. It applies to the portion of your estate that exceeds the annual exemption (about $13.61 million per person in 2024). Your gross estate includes real estate, bank accounts, investments, retirement accounts, life insurance you own, business interests, and other assets. Deductions (debts, expenses, charitable bequests, and marital deduction) reduce the taxable amount. This calculator estimates the federal tax and a rough state estimate; actual results depend on your situation and state rules.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "Gross estate" %}

{% trans "Total value of all assets before deductions (real estate, accounts, investments, life insurance you own, business interests, etc.)." %}

{% trans "Deductions and taxable estate" %}

{% trans "Debts, funeral and administrative expenses, charitable bequests, and marital deduction reduce the gross estate. Taxable estate = gross minus deductions (combined with prior taxable gifts for the tax calculation)." %}

{% trans "Exemption and net to heirs" %}

{% trans "The exemption (e.g. $13.61 million in 2024) is the amount not subject to tax. Married couples may use portability. Net to heirs is what remains after federal and state estate taxes." %}
