{% trans "What is the Finance Calculator?" %}

{% blocktrans %}This calculator computes compound interest: how much an initial amount (principal) grows when invested at a fixed annual rate over a number of years. You see future value, total interest earned, and how balance grows year by year. Interest is reinvested each period, so you earn interest on interest.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Compound Interest Formula" %}

{% trans "The formula for future value with annual compounding:" %}

FV = P × (1 + r)^t

{% trans "FV = future value, P = principal, r = annual rate (as decimal, e.g. 0.05 for 5%), t = time in years" %}

{% trans "Example:" %}

{% trans "$10,000 at 5% for 10 years: FV = 10000 × (1.05)^10 ≈ $16,289. Total interest ≈ $6,289." %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "Principal" %}

{% trans "The initial amount you invest or deposit. Interest is earned on this amount and on previously earned interest (compound interest)." %}

{% trans "Compound interest" %}

{% trans "Interest is added to the balance each period, so you earn interest on interest. Over long periods this growth becomes substantial." %}

{% trans "Interest %" %}

{% trans "The percentage of your future value that came from interest (vs principal). Higher rate or longer time increases this share." %}
