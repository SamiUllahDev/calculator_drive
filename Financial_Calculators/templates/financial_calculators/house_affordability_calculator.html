{% trans "What is House Affordability?" %}

{% blocktrans %}House affordability is the maximum home price you can afford based on your income, existing debts, down payment, and loan terms. Lenders use debt-to-income (DTI) ratios to ensure you can repay the loan. The 28/36 rule is common: no more than 28% of gross income on housing (front-end DTI) and no more than 36% on all debts combined (back-end DTI).{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Front-End vs Back-End DTI" %}

{% trans "Front-End DTI (28% guideline)" %}

{% trans "Housing payment (P&I, taxes, insurance, PMI, HOA) ÷ gross monthly income. Lenders prefer this under 28%." %}

{% trans "Back-End DTI (36% guideline)" %}

{% trans "All monthly debts (housing + car, student loans, credit cards, etc.) ÷ gross monthly income. Lenders prefer this under 36%." %}

{% trans "Formulas Used" %}

{% trans "Max housing payment" %}

min(28% × monthly income, 36% × monthly income − monthly debts)

{% trans "Monthly P&I" %}

P&I = Loan × [ r(1+r)^n ] / [ (1+r)^n − 1 ]

r = monthly rate, n = number of payments.
