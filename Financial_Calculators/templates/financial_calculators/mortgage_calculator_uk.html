{% trans "What is a UK mortgage?" %}

{% trans "A mortgage is a loan used to buy a property. In the UK, mortgages are commonly either repayment (capital + interest) or interest-only (interest payments only, with the capital repaid at the end)." %}

{% trans "How to Use" %}

  1. {% trans "Enter the property price and your deposit." %}
  2. {% trans "Choose the term and annual interest rate." %}
  3. {% trans "Select repayment or interest-only." %}
  4. {% trans "Click Calculate to see the monthly payment and schedule." %}

{% trans "What is LTV?" %}

{% trans "Loan-to-Value (LTV) is the loan amount divided by the property price. A higher deposit reduces LTV and can help you access better rates." %}

{% trans "Mortgage Formulas" %}

{% trans "Loan Amount" %}:
{% trans "Loan = Property Price − Deposit" %}

{% trans "Repayment (monthly payment)" %}:
M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1]

{% trans "Interest-only (monthly payment)" %}:
M = P × r

{% trans "Where P is loan amount, r is monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12), and n is number of months." %}
