{% trans "What is Mortgage Payoff?" %}

{% blocktrans %}Mortgage payoff refers to paying off your home loan before the end of its original term. By making extra payments — whether monthly, yearly, or as a lump sum — you reduce the principal balance faster, which means less interest accumulates over time. This can save you tens of thousands of dollars and free you from mortgage debt years earlier than planned.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Total Savings = Original Interest − Payoff Interest" %}

{% blocktrans %}The key principle is simple: every extra dollar you pay goes directly toward reducing your principal balance. Since interest is calculated on the remaining balance, a lower balance means less interest on every future payment — creating a compounding savings effect over the life of the loan.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Payoff Strategies" %}

{% trans "Extra Monthly Payments" %}

{% trans "Adding a fixed amount to your monthly payment is the most common strategy. Even $200-$500 extra per month can save $50,000-$100,000+ in interest and cut 5-10 years off a 30-year mortgage. This method is easy to budget for and provides consistent acceleration." %}

{% trans "Biweekly Payments" %}

{% trans "Instead of 12 monthly payments, you pay half your payment every two weeks. With 52 weeks per year, this results in 26 half-payments — the equivalent of 13 full payments instead of 12. That extra payment each year can shave 4-6 years off a 30-year loan with minimal effort." %}

{% trans "Lump Sum Payments" %}

{% trans "One-time payments from bonuses, tax refunds, or inheritance can create a dramatic reduction in your loan balance. A single $10,000 lump sum early in a 30-year mortgage can save over $20,000 in interest. The earlier you make the lump sum, the greater the impact." %}

{% trans "Combined Strategy" %}

{% trans "The most powerful approach combines all strategies: make extra monthly payments, switch to biweekly, and apply windfalls as lump sums. Used together, you could pay off a 30-year mortgage in 15-18 years and save hundreds of thousands in interest." %}
