{% trans "What is the Rent Affordability Calculator?" %}

{% blocktrans %}This calculator estimates how much rent you can afford based on your gross monthly income, existing debt payments, and common budgeting rules. It uses the 30%% rule (rent should not exceed 30%% of gross income), the 28/36 rule, and the 50/30/20 framework to give you a range of affordable rent amounts.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}You enter your income, monthly debt (car, loans, credit cards), estimated utilities, and a preferred rent percentage. The result shows recommended rent, budget breakdown, and rent by different guidelines.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Key Rules" %}

{% trans "30% Rule" %}

{% trans "Rent should not exceed 30% of your gross monthly income. This is the most common guideline for rent affordability." %}

{% trans "28/36 Rule" %}

{% trans "Housing (rent + utilities) should not exceed 28% of gross income; total debt (housing + other debts) should not exceed 36%." %}

{% trans "50/30/20 Rule" %}

{% trans "50% of take-home pay for needs (rent, utilities, food), 30% for wants, 20% for savings. Rent is part of the 50% needs bucket." %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "Gross income" %}

{% trans "Income before taxes. The 30% rule uses gross (not take-home) income for consistency with how landlords often qualify tenants." %}

{% trans "Monthly obligations" %}

{% trans "Car payments, student loans, credit card minimums, and other debt. Higher debt reduces how much you can safely put toward rent under the 36% rule." %}

{% trans "After rent" %}

{% trans "Estimated remaining after rent, utilities, and debt. This is for food, savings, and other expenses; a positive number is essential." %}

{% trans "How to Use This Calculator" %}

  1. {% trans "Enter your gross monthly income (before taxes). Add any additional monthly income if needed." %}
  2. {% trans "Enter your monthly debt payments: car, student loans, credit cards, other." %}
  3. {% trans "Choose a rent budget rule (e.g. 30%) and estimated utilities." %}
  4. {% trans "Click Calculate to see recommended rent, breakdown chart, and rent by guideline." %}
  5. {% trans "Use the table and charts to compare conservative vs maximum rent levels." %}

{% trans "Understanding Your Results" %}

{% trans "Recommended monthly rent" %}

{% trans "Based on the 30% rule by default. You can choose a different percentage in the form to see a custom recommendation." %}

{% trans "Budget breakdown" %}

{% trans "Shows how your income is allocated: rent, utilities, debt payments, and remaining. Remaining should cover food, savings, and discretionary spending." %}

{% trans "Rent by rule" %}

{% trans "Different guidelines (25% to 35%) give different monthly rent amounts. Conservative (25%) leaves more buffer; 35% is often considered the upper limit." %}

{% trans "Understanding the Charts" %}

{% trans "Budget Breakdown" %}

{% trans "A doughnut chart showing rent, utilities, debt payments, and remaining after those obligations. Helps you see at a glance how much is left for other expenses." %}

{% trans "Rent by Rule (%)" %}

{% trans "Bar chart comparing monthly rent at 25%%, 28%%, 30%%, 33%%, and 35%% of gross income. Use it to pick a rent level that fits your budget." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "Results are estimates. Taxes and take-home pay are approximated; actual budgets depend on your situation." %}
  • {% trans "Factor in renters insurance, parking, and moving costs (first month, last month, security deposit) when planning." %}
  • {% trans "Keeping rent at or below 30%% of gross income helps maintain flexibility for savings and emergencies." %}
