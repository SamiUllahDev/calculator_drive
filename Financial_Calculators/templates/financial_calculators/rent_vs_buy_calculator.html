{% trans "What is Rent vs Buy?" %}

{% trans "The rent vs buy decision compares the long-term financial outcome of renting a home and investing the difference versus buying a home with a mortgage. Both paths have costs and build wealth in different ways: buying builds equity in a property; renting can free up cash to invest in the market." %}

{% trans "Key Factors" %}

  • {% trans "How long you plan to stay (time horizon)" %}
  • {% trans "Home price, down payment, and mortgage rate" %}
  • {% trans "Property taxes, insurance, HOA, and maintenance" %}
  • {% trans "Rent level and expected rent increases" %}
  • {% trans "Expected home appreciation vs investment returns" %}

{% trans "How to Use This Calculator" %}

  1. {% trans "Enter the home price and your down payment percentage" %}
  2. {% trans "Enter interest rate, loan term, property tax, insurance, HOA, and maintenance" %}
  3. {% trans "Enter expected home appreciation rate" %}
  4. {% trans "Enter monthly rent, rent increase rate, and renters insurance" %}
  5. {% trans "Set the time period (years) and expected investment return if you rent" %}

{% trans "Understanding Your Results" %}

{% trans "Better Option" %}

{% trans "Whether buying or renting comes out ahead in net wealth over your chosen period. The difference is the gap in net position (equity or investments minus total costs)." %}

{% trans "Net Position" %}

{% trans "For buying: home equity minus total costs paid. For renting: investment value minus total rent and insurance paid. The higher net position is the financially better outcome." %}

{% trans "Breakeven Year" %}

{% trans "The first year when buying's net position exceeds renting's. Before that, renting is ahead; after that, buying is ahead (for the assumptions used)." %}

{% trans "Understanding the Charts" %}

{% trans "The line chart shows how much wealth each path builds over your chosen period. The blue line is home equity if you buy; the amber line is the value of investments if you rent and invest the difference. Where the lines cross and which line is higher at the end helps you see the impact of appreciation, rent growth, and investment return." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "Closing costs when buying and selling are not included; add a few years to break even in practice." %}
  • {% trans "This is a financial comparison only. Lifestyle, job stability, and flexibility matter too." %}
