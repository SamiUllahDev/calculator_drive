{% trans "What is a Required Minimum Distribution?" %}

{% trans "An RMD is the minimum amount you must withdraw from tax-deferred retirement accounts (Traditional IRA, 401(k), 403(b), SEP IRA, SIMPLE IRA) each year once you reach the required age. The IRS requires this so you pay taxes on tax-deferred savings. Roth IRAs do not require RMDs during your lifetime." %}

{% trans "What Your Results Mean" %}

{% trans "Current Year RMD & Distribution Period" %}

{% trans "The amount you must withdraw this year. The distribution period is your life expectancy factor from the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table (balance ÷ factor = RMD)." %}

{% trans "Projected RMDs and account balance over time. Before your RMD start age, no RMD is required; after that, RMD and growth are applied each year." %}

{% trans "Formulas & SECURE Act 2.0" %}

{% trans "RMD = Prior year-end balance ÷ life expectancy factor (IRS Uniform Lifetime Table). SECURE Act 2.0: Born 1950 or earlier start at 72; 1951–1959 at 73; 1960+ at 75. Deadline: December 31 each year (first RMD can delay to April 1 of next year, but then two RMDs that year)." %}

{% trans "Missing an RMD: 25%% excise tax on the shortfall (may be reduced to 10%% if corrected promptly)." %}
