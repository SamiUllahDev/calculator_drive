{% trans "What is a Required Minimum Distribution?" %}

{% trans "A Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) is the minimum amount you must withdraw from your retirement account each year once you reach a certain age. The IRS requires these withdrawals to ensure you pay taxes on your tax-deferred retirement savings." %}

{% trans "Traditional IRAs require RMDs" %}
{% trans "401(k) and 403(b) plans require RMDs" %}
{% trans "Roth IRAs do NOT require RMDs during your lifetime" %}

{% trans "RMD Penalties" %}

{% trans "25% Penalty for Missing RMDs" %}

{% trans "If you don't take your full RMD by the deadline, you'll owe a 25% excise tax on the amount not withdrawn. This can be reduced to 10% if corrected promptly." %}

{% trans "Deadline" %}

{% trans "RMDs must be taken by December 31st each year. First-year RMDs can be delayed until April 1st of the following year (but you'll need to take two RMDs that year)." %}

{% trans "Frequently Asked Questions" %}

{% trans "How is my RMD calculated?" %}

{% trans "Your RMD is calculated by dividing your prior year-end account balance by your life expectancy factor from the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. For example, if your balance was $500,000 and your factor is 26.5, your RMD would be $18,868." %}

{% trans "Can I withdraw more than my RMD?" %}

{% trans "Yes! The RMD is the minimum you must withdraw. You can always take more. However, any excess cannot be applied to future years' RMDs. Extra withdrawals will be taxed as ordinary income." %}

{% trans "What if I have multiple IRAs?" %}

{% trans "You must calculate RMDs separately for each Traditional IRA you own. However, you can take the total amount from any one or combination of your IRAs. 401(k) RMDs must be taken from each account separately." %}
