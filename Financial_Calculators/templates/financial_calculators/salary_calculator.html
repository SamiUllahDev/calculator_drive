{% trans "What is the Salary Calculator?" %}

{% blocktrans %}The Salary Calculator helps you convert pay between hourly, daily, weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, monthly, and annual amounts. It also lets you calculate salary raises (percentage or fixed amount), compare two job offers on an annual basis, and compute overtime pay for a given week. All conversions use the hours per week and weeks per year you enter (default 40 and 52).{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Calculator Modes" %}

{% trans "Convert" %}

{% trans "Enter one pay amount and its period; see the equivalent in all other periods (hourly, daily, weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, monthly, annual)." %}

{% trans "Raise" %}

{% trans "Enter current annual salary and a raise as a percentage or dollar amount. Results show new salary, raise amount, percentage increase, and approximate monthly and per-paycheck increase." %}

{% trans "Compare" %}

{% trans "Enter two offers (amount and period for each). The calculator converts both to annual and shows which is better and by how much." %}

{% trans "Overtime" %}

{% trans "Enter hourly rate, regular hours, overtime hours, and OT multiplier (e.g. 1.5x). Results show regular pay, overtime pay, total weekly pay, and an estimated monthly projection." %}

{% trans "How to Use" %}

  1. {% trans "Choose a mode: Convert, Raise, Compare, or Overtime." %}
  2. {% trans "Fill in the fields for that mode (amount, period, hours/week, weeks/year for Convert; current salary and raise for Raise; two offers for Compare; rate and hours for Overtime)." %}
  3. {% trans "Click 'Calculate Salary' to see your results. The main result appears in the hero card; the breakdown and chart (when available) appear below." %}
  4. {% trans "Scroll to the results area to see the full breakdown and any chart." %}

{% trans "Understanding Results and Charts" %}

{% trans "The hero card shows the primary result for the selected mode (e.g. annual salary for Convert, new salary for Raise). The chart visualizes the breakdown: bar chart for conversions or offer comparison, doughnut for raise (current vs raise) or overtime (regular vs overtime). All amounts are in your chosen currency (USD by default)." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "Conversions assume a fixed schedule (e.g. 40 hours/week, 52 weeks/year). Actual pay may vary with overtime, unpaid leave, or different schedules." %}
  • {% trans "Overtime: In the U.S., non-exempt employees typically receive at least 1.5× regular rate for hours over 40 per workweek (FLSA). State rules may differ." %}
  • {% trans "This tool is for estimation only. Tax, benefits, and deductions are not included." %}
