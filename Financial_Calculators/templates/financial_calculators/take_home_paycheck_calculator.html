{% trans "What is the Take Home Paycheck Calculator?" %}

{% blocktrans %}This calculator estimates your net (take-home) pay per paycheck after federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security, Medicare, and your pre-tax and post-tax deductions. It uses current tax brackets and FICA limits (e.g. 2024) and lets you choose pay frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, monthly, or annual) so you see results in the same period you get paid.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

{% trans "Federal & State Tax" %}

{% trans "Withholding is estimated from tax brackets and your filing status. State rates vary (some states have no income tax)." %}

{% trans "FICA (Social Security & Medicare)" %}

{% trans "Social Security is 6.2% up to the wage base; Medicare is 1.45% (plus an additional 0.9% above a threshold). These apply to gross wages." %}

{% trans "Pre-Tax Deductions" %}

{% trans "401(k), health insurance, HSA/FSA, etc. reduce taxable income for federal (and often state) tax but not for FICA." %}

{% trans "How to Use" %}

  1. {% trans "Enter your gross pay per paycheck and select pay frequency." %}
  2. {% trans "Choose filing status and state." %}
  3. {% trans "Enter any pre-tax deductions (401(k), health, HSA/FSA, other) and post-tax deductions per paycheck." %}
  4. {% trans "Click 'Calculate Take-Home Pay' to see net pay, breakdown, annual projections, and the chart." %}

{% trans "Understanding Results and Charts" %}

{% trans "The hero card shows your take-home pay per paycheck. The doughnut chart shows how each paycheck is split: take-home (green), federal tax, state tax, Social Security, Medicare, and pre-tax/post-tax deductions. The bar and annual summary help you see effective tax rate and yearly totals." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "This is an estimate. Actual withholding depends on your W-4, state forms, and employer setup." %}
  • {% trans "Tax brackets and FICA limits are for illustration (e.g. 2024). Use current IRS and state data for exact figures." %}
  • {% trans "Pre-tax deductions reduce federal/state taxable income but not FICA in most cases." %}
