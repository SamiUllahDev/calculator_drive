{% trans "What is a VA Loan?" %}

{% blocktrans %}A VA loan is a mortgage guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. It is available to eligible veterans, active-duty service members, and qualifying surviving spouses. VA loans are issued by private lenders but backed by the federal government, which allows lenders to offer favorable terms such as no down payment and no private mortgage insurance (PMI).{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}The VA does not lend money directly; it provides a guarantee that protects the lender if the borrower defaults. This guarantee enables lenders to offer competitive interest rates and flexible qualification requirements. The VA funding fee helps sustain the program and can be paid at closing or financed into the loan amount.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "VA Loan Benefits" %}

{% trans "No Down Payment" %}

{% trans "Qualified borrowers can finance up to 100% of the home's value. No down payment is required, though you may choose to put money down to reduce the loan amount and funding fee." %}

{% trans "No PMI" %}

{% trans "Unlike conventional loans, VA loans do not require Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI), even with zero down. This can save hundreds of dollars per month compared to other loan types." %}

{% trans "Competitive Rates & Limited Closing Costs" %}

{% trans "Lenders often offer competitive interest rates on VA loans. VA also limits certain closing costs that lenders can charge to veterans." %}

{% trans "No Prepayment Penalties & Assumable Loans" %}

{% trans "You can pay off your VA loan early without penalty. VA loans are also assumable, meaning a qualified buyer can take over your loan and its terms." %}

{% trans "VA Funding Fee" %}

{% blocktrans %}The VA funding fee is a one-time fee that helps offset the cost of the program to taxpayers. The fee is based on your down payment (or equity), whether it's your first VA loan or a subsequent use, and whether you're in the regular military or Reserves/National Guard. Veterans with service-connected disabilities may be exempt from the funding fee. The fee can be paid at closing or financed into the loan.{% endblocktrans %}
