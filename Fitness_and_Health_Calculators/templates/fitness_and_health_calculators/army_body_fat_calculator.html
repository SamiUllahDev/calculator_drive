{% trans "Complete User Guide" %}

{% trans "What is the Army Body Fat Calculator?" %}

{% trans "The US Army Body Fat Calculator uses a specific measurement-based formula to estimate body fat percentage. This method is used by the US Army to determine if service members meet body composition standards required for military service." %}

{% trans "Unlike BMI, this calculator uses actual body measurements (neck, waist, and hip circumference) along with height to provide a more accurate assessment of body fat percentage." %}

{% trans "US Army Body Fat Standards" %}

{% trans "Males" %}

{% trans "Ages 17-20: Max 20% | Ages 21-27: Max 22% | Ages 28-39: Max 24% | Ages 40+: Max 26%" %}

{% trans "Females" %}

{% trans "Ages 17-20: Max 30% | Ages 21-27: Max 32% | Ages 28-39: Max 34% | Ages 40+: Max 36%" %}

{% trans "How to Use This Calculator" %}

  1. {% trans "Select your gender (Male or Female)" %}
  2. {% trans "Enter your age (17-65 years)" %}
  3. {% trans "Enter your height in inches" %}
  4. {% trans "Measure and enter your neck circumference (just below the larynx)" %}
  5. {% trans "Measure and enter your waist circumference (at the navel level)" %}
  6. {% trans "For females, measure and enter your hip circumference (widest point)" %}
  7. {% trans "Click 'Calculate Body Fat' to see your results" %}

{% trans "How to Take Measurements" %}

{% trans "Neck Measurement" %}

{% trans "Measure around the neck, just below the larynx (Adam's apple). Keep the tape measure horizontal and snug but not tight." %}

{% trans "Waist Measurement" %}

{% trans "Measure at the navel level (belly button), not at the narrowest point. Stand straight, breathe normally, and measure at the end of a normal breath." %}

{% trans "Hip Measurement (Females Only)" %}

{% trans "Measure around the hips at the widest point. This is typically around the buttocks area. Keep the tape measure horizontal." %}

{% trans "US Army Body Fat Formula" %}

{% trans "For Males:" %}

{% trans "Body Fat % = 86.010 × log10(waist - neck) - 70.041 × log10(height) + 36.76" %}

{% trans "For Females:" %}

{% trans "Body Fat % = 163.205 × log10(waist + hip - neck) - 97.684 × log10(height) - 78.387" %}

{% trans "All measurements are in inches. The formula uses logarithmic calculations to estimate body fat percentage based on body circumference measurements." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "This calculator uses the US Army body fat estimation formula. Actual body fat percentage may vary when measured with more precise methods like DEXA scan or hydrostatic weighing." %}
  • {% trans "Accurate measurements are crucial. Measure at the same time of day, preferably in the morning before eating." %}
  • {% trans "This calculator is for educational and informational purposes only. Official Army body fat assessments are conducted by trained personnel using standardized procedures." %}
  • {% trans "Body fat standards may change. Always refer to the latest official US Army regulations for current requirements." %}
