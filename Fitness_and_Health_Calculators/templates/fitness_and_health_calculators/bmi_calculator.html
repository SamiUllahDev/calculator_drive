{% trans "What is BMI?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement of a person's leanness or corpulence based on their height and weight, and is intended to quantify tissue mass. It is widely used as a general indicator of whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. However, BMI does not directly measure body fat — for a more accurate assessment of your fat percentage, consider using a{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Body Fat Calculator" %}. {% blocktrans trimmed %}If you want to know the recommended weight for your height and frame, our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Ideal Weight Calculator" %} {% trans "can also help." %}

{% trans "BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m)²" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}These ranges of BMI vary based on factors such as region and age, and are sometimes further divided into subcategories such as severely underweight or very severely obese. Being overweight or underweight can have significant health effects. To better understand your overall body composition, you may also want to check your{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Lean Body Mass" %} {% trans "and" %} {% trans "Body Surface Area" %}.

{% trans "BMI Formula" %}

{% trans "The equations used for calculating BMI in the International System of Units (SI) and the US customary system (USC):" %}

{% trans "SI, Metric Units:" %}

BMI = mass (kg) / height² (m)

{% trans "USC Units:" %}

BMI = 703 × mass (lbs) / height² (in)
