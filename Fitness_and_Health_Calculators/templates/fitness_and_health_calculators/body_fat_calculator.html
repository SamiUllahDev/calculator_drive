{% trans "Complete User Guide" %}

{% trans "What is Body Fat Percentage?" %}

{% trans "Body fat percentage is the proportion of fat mass to total body weight. Unlike BMI, which only considers weight and height, body fat percentage provides a more accurate assessment of body composition by distinguishing between fat mass and lean mass (muscle, bone, organs, water)." %}

{% trans "Knowing your body fat percentage helps you understand your health status better than weight alone, as it accounts for muscle mass and overall body composition." %}

{% trans "US Navy Body Fat Method" %}

{% trans "This calculator uses the US Navy body fat estimation method, which is one of the most accurate measurement-based formulas available. It uses body circumference measurements (neck, waist, and hip for women) along with height to estimate body fat percentage." %}

{% trans "For Men:" %}

{% trans "Body Fat % = 495 / (1.0324 - 0.19077 × log10(waist - neck) + 0.15456 × log10(height)) - 450" %}

{% trans "For Women:" %}

{% trans "Body Fat % = 495 / (1.29579 - 0.35004 × log10(waist + hip - neck) + 0.22100 × log10(height)) - 450" %}

{% trans "All measurements are in centimeters. The formula uses logarithmic calculations to provide accurate estimates based on body circumference measurements." %}

{% trans "Body Fat Categories" %}

{% trans "Essential Fat" %}

{% trans "Men: 2-5% | Women: 10-13%" %}

{% trans "The minimum amount of body fat necessary for basic physiological functions. Below this level can be dangerous." %}

{% trans "Athletes" %}

{% trans "Men: 6-13% | Women: 14-20%" %}

{% trans "Very lean body fat levels typical of competitive athletes. Excellent muscle definition." %}

{% trans "Fitness" %}

{% trans "Men: 14-17% | Women: 21-24%" %}

{% trans "Good body fat level for active individuals. Visible muscle definition, healthy appearance." %}

{% trans "Average" %}

{% trans "Men: 18-24% | Women: 25-31%" %}

{% trans "Average body fat level for the general population. May have some excess fat but generally healthy." %}

{% trans "Obese" %}

{% trans "Men: >25% | Women: >32%" %}

{% trans "Excess body fat that may increase health risks. Consider consulting with a healthcare provider." %}

{% trans "How to Take Measurements" %}

{% trans "Neck Measurement" %}

{% trans "Measure just below the larynx (Adam's apple) with the tape sloping slightly downward at the front. Keep the tape horizontal and snug but not tight." %}

{% trans "Waist Measurement" %}

{% trans "Measure at your navel level (belly button), not at the narrowest point. Stand straight, breathe normally, and measure at the end of a normal breath. Keep the tape level and parallel to the floor." %}

{% trans "Hip Measurement (Females Only)" %}

{% trans "Measure around the hips at the widest point. This is typically around the buttocks area. Keep the tape measure horizontal and parallel to the floor." %}

{% trans "Tip:" %} {% trans "For best accuracy, take measurements in the morning before eating, and measure multiple times to ensure consistency." %}

{% trans "Understanding Your Results" %}

{% trans "Body Fat Percentage" %}

{% trans "Your estimated body fat percentage based on the US Navy formula. This is an estimate and may vary from other measurement methods like DEXA scan or hydrostatic weighing." %}

{% trans "Fat Mass" %}

{% trans "The total weight of fat in your body, calculated as a percentage of your total body weight." %}

{% trans "Lean Mass" %}

{% trans "The total weight of everything except fat: muscle, bone, organs, and water. Higher lean mass generally indicates better fitness and health." %}

{% trans "Ideal Weight" %}

{% trans "An estimated weight at which you would have an ideal body fat percentage (10-20% for men, 18-28% for women) while maintaining your current lean mass." %}

{% trans "Category" %}

{% trans "Your body fat classification based on gender-specific ranges. This helps you understand where you stand relative to health and fitness standards." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

  • {% trans "This calculator provides ESTIMATES based on body measurements. Actual body fat percentage may vary when measured with more precise methods like DEXA scan, hydrostatic weighing, or BodPod." %}
  • {% trans "Accurate measurements are crucial. Measure at the same time of day, preferably in the morning before eating, and ensure the tape measure is level and snug but not tight." %}
  • {% trans "Body fat percentage is just one indicator of health. Consider other factors like blood pressure, cholesterol, blood sugar, and overall fitness level." %}
