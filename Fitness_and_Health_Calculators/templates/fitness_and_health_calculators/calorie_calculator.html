{% trans "What are Calories?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}A calorie is a unit of energy. In nutrition, calories refer to the energy people get from the food and drink they consume, and the energy they use in physical activity. The number of calories you need each day depends on your age, gender, height, weight, and activity level. You can also check your{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Body Mass Index (BMI)" %} {% trans "to see where you stand." %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Understanding your daily calorie needs is essential for achieving your fitness goals, whether you want to lose weight, maintain your current weight, or gain muscle. Your{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)" %} {% blocktrans trimmed %}and{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)" %} {% trans "are key factors in determining your calorie needs." %}

{% trans "Understanding BMR and TDEE" %}

{% trans "BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}The number of calories your body burns at rest to maintain basic life functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production. Calculate yours with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "BMR Calculator" %}.

{% trans "TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Your total daily calorie burn, including BMR plus calories burned through physical activity and exercise. This is the number of calories you need to maintain your current weight. Use our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "TDEE Calculator" %} {% trans "for a detailed breakdown." %}

{% trans "Target Calories" %}

{% trans "Your TDEE adjusted based on your goal. A deficit (negative adjustment) helps you lose weight, while a surplus (positive adjustment) helps you gain weight." %}

{% trans "Calorie Goals Explained" %}

{% trans "Lose Weight Fast (-1000 cal):" %} {% trans "Aggressive weight loss. May be difficult to maintain long-term. Consult a healthcare provider." %}

{% trans "Lose Weight (-500 cal):" %} {% trans "Moderate weight loss. Typically results in ~0.5 kg (1 lb) per week. Sustainable for most people." %}

{% trans "Lose Weight Slowly (-250 cal):" %} {% trans "Gradual weight loss. Typically results in ~0.25 kg (0.5 lb) per week. Very sustainable." %}

{% trans "Maintain Weight (0 cal):" %} {% trans "Eat at your TDEE level to maintain your current weight." %}

{% trans "Gain Weight Slowly (+250 cal):" %} {% trans "Gradual weight gain. Good for lean muscle building." %}

{% trans "Gain Weight (+500 cal):" %} {% trans "Moderate weight gain. Typically results in ~0.5 kg (1 lb) per week." %}

{% trans "Gain Weight Fast (+1000 cal):" %} {% trans "Aggressive weight gain. May result in more fat gain than muscle." %}

{% trans "Understanding Macronutrients" %}

{% trans "Protein (4 calories per gram)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Essential for muscle repair and growth. Higher protein intake is recommended for weight loss (35%) and muscle gain (30%). Find your exact needs with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Protein Calculator" %}.

{% trans "Carbohydrates (4 calories per gram)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Primary energy source. Higher carb intake (45%) is recommended for weight gain, moderate (35-40%) for maintenance and weight loss. Use our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Carbohydrate Calculator" %} {% trans "for personalized recommendations." %}

{% trans "Fat (9 calories per gram)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Important for hormone production and nutrient absorption. Typically 25-30% of total calories. Check your ideal intake with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Fat Intake Calculator" %}.
