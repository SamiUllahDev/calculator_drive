{% trans "What is a Healthy Weight?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}A healthy weight is a weight range that is associated with the lowest risk of health problems. It's typically defined using Body Mass Index (BMI), which is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. Calculate your exact BMI value with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "BMI Calculator" %}.

{% blocktrans trimmed %}The healthy weight range corresponds to a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9. This range is associated with the lowest risk of developing weight-related health conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. For a more specific target, try our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Ideal Weight Calculator" %}.

{% trans "BMI Categories Explained" %}

{% trans "Underweight (BMI < 18.5)" %}

{% trans "May indicate insufficient body weight for optimal health. Can be associated with nutritional deficiencies, weakened immune system, and bone loss." %}

{% trans "Normal Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Associated with the lowest risk of health problems. This is the ideal weight range for most people. Verify your body composition with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Body Fat Calculator" %} {% trans "for a more complete picture." %}

{% trans "Overweight (BMI 25-29.9)" %}

{% trans "May increase the risk of developing health problems such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and heart disease." %}

{% trans "Obese (BMI ≥ 30)" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Significantly increases the risk of serious health problems including heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, certain cancers, and sleep apnea. Use our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "TDEE Calculator" %} {% blocktrans trimmed %}and{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Calorie Calculator" %} {% trans "to create a sustainable weight management plan." %}

{% trans "Factors Affecting Healthy Weight" %}

{% trans "Height:" %} {% trans "Taller individuals naturally weigh more than shorter individuals. The calculator adjusts weight ranges based on your height." %}

{% trans "Body Composition:" %} {% blocktrans trimmed %}Muscle weighs more than fat. Athletes with high muscle mass may have a higher BMI but still be healthy. Assess this with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Body Fat Calculator" %} {% trans "and" %} {% trans "Lean Body Mass Calculator" %}.

{% trans "Age:" %} {% blocktrans trimmed %}Metabolism changes with age. Older adults may need to adjust their weight goals based on health conditions and activity levels. Check your metabolic rate with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "BMR Calculator" %}.

{% trans "Gender:" %} {% trans "Men and women have different body compositions. Men typically have more muscle mass, which affects healthy weight ranges." %}

{% trans "Health Conditions:" %} {% trans "Certain medical conditions may require different weight targets. Always consult with healthcare providers for personalized recommendations." %}

{% trans "Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Weight" %}

{% trans "Balanced Diet:" %} {% blocktrans trimmed %}Eat a variety of nutrient-dense foods including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Plan your macros with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Macro Calculator" %}.

{% trans "Regular Exercise:" %} {% blocktrans trimmed %}Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week, including both cardio and strength training. Track your activity with our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Calories Burned Calculator" %}.

{% trans "Portion Control:" %} {% trans "Be mindful of portion sizes and avoid overeating. Use smaller plates and listen to your body's hunger cues." %}
