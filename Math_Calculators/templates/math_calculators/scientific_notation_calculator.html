{% trans "What is Scientific Notation?" %}

{% blocktrans %}Scientific notation writes numbers as a mantissa (between 1 and 10, or 0) times a power of 10. For example, 1,500,000 = 1.5 × 10^6. It is used in science and engineering to handle very large numbers (like distances in space) and very small numbers (like atomic sizes) in a compact form.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Key Concepts" %}

  • {% trans "Mantissa (coefficient):" %} {% trans "A number usually between 1 and 10 (or 0). Example: in 1.5 × 10^6, the mantissa is 1.5." %}
  • {% trans "Exponent:" %} {% trans "The power of 10. Example: in 1.5 × 10^6, the exponent is 6, so the value is 1.5 × 1,000,000 = 1,500,000." %}
  • {% trans "E-notation:" %} {% trans "Shorthand such as 1.5e6 or 1.5E6 meaning 1.5 × 10^6. Negative exponents for small numbers: 1.5e-3 = 0.0015." %}
