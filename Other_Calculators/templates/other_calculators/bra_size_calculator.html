{% trans "Bra size consists of two components: the band size (number) and the cup size (letter). The band size is the measurement around your ribcage, typically ranging from 28 to 50. The cup size represents the difference between your bust and band measurements, ranging from AA to N." %}

{% trans "How to Measure" %}

{% trans "Underbust Measurement" %}

{% trans "Measure around your ribcage, directly under your bust. Keep the tape measure level and snug but not tight. This measurement determines your band size." %}

{% trans "Bust Measurement" %}

{% trans "Measure around the fullest part of your bust, typically at nipple level. Keep the tape measure level and parallel to the ground. Wear a non-padded bra for accurate measurement." %}

{% trans "Important Notes" %}

{% trans "Frequently Asked Questions" %}

{% trans "How do I measure my bra size?" %}

{% trans "Measure your underbust (ribcage) snugly around your torso, and your bust at the fullest point. The difference between these measurements determines your cup size, while your underbust measurement determines your band size." %}

{% trans "What is a sister size?" %}

{% trans "Sister sizes are bra sizes with the same cup volume but different band and cup combinations. For example, 34C, 32D, and 36B are sister sizes. They can be useful if your regular size is not available." %}

{% trans "How do I convert between US and UK bra sizes?" %}

{% trans "US and UK bra sizes use the same band sizes, but cup sizes differ after D. US uses D, DD, DDD, G, H, while UK uses D, DD, E, F, FF, G, GG, H. Use our size conversion tool for accurate conversions." %}

{% trans "What is the difference between band size and cup size?" %}

{% trans "Band size is the measurement around your ribcage (underbust), typically 28-50. Cup size is determined by the difference between your bust and band measurements, ranging from AA to N." %}

{% trans "Should I measure with or without a bra?" %}

{% trans "For the most accurate measurement, wear a non-padded, well-fitting bra. The bra should not add extra volume. Measure your underbust snugly and your bust at the fullest point while wearing the bra." %}

{% trans "Why does my bra size vary between brands?" %}

{% trans "Bra sizing can vary between brands due to different manufacturing standards, materials, and fit preferences. Always try on bras when possible, and use sister sizes if your regular size doesn't fit in a particular brand." %}
