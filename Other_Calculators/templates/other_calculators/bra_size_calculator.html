{% trans "How to Measure Your Bra Size at Home" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Your bra size consists of two parts: the band size (a number) and the cup size (a letter). The band size is your underbust measurement rounded to the nearest even number. The cup size is determined by the difference between your bust and band measurements. Getting accurate measurements is the first step to finding a comfortable, well-fitting bra. For converting between inches and centimeters, our{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Conversion Calculator" %} {% trans "can help." %}

{% trans "Sizing Systems Explained: US vs UK vs EU vs FR" %}

{% trans "US / CA / MX Sizing" %}

{% trans "Band sizes 28-50. Cup progression: AA, A, B, C, D, DD, DDD, G, H, I, J, K, L, M, N. Most common in North America." %}

{% trans "UK / PK / IN / NZ / AU / ZA Sizing" %}

{% trans "Same band sizes. Different cup progression after D: DD, E, F, FF, G, GG, H, HH, J, JJ. Widely used in Commonwealth countries." %}

{% trans "EU / IT / ES / DE / JP / CN / BR Sizing" %}

{% trans "Same band sizes as US. Cups: AA, A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, L, M, N. Alphabetical progression." %}

{% trans "FR (France) Sizing" %}

{% trans "Uses centimeter-based band sizes (add 15 to US/UK band). Cups same as EU. Example: US 34C = FR 49C." %}

{% trans "What Are Sister Sizes?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Sister sizes are bra sizes that share the same cup volume but have different band and cup letter combinations. For example, 34C, 32D, and 36B are all sister sizes — they hold the same amount of breast tissue but distribute support differently. Understanding your body measurements is also important for overall health — check your{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "BMI" %} {% trans "or" %} {% trans "Body Type" %} {% trans "for a complete picture." %}
