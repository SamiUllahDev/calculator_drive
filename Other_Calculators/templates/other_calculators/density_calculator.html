{% trans "What is Density?" %}

{% blocktrans %}Density is a physical property that measures the mass per unit volume of a substance. It tells you how tightly packed the matter is in a given space. The formula is simple: Density = Mass ÷ Volume (ρ = m / V). Different materials have different densities — water is 1,000 kg/m³, aluminum is 2,700 kg/m³, and gold is 19,300 kg/m³.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "All Formulas at a Glance" %}

{% trans "Density" %}: ρ = m / V

{% trans "Mass" %}: m = ρ × V

{% trans "Volume" %}: V = m / ρ
