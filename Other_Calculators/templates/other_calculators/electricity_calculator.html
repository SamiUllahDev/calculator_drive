{% trans "Ohm's Law and Power Formulas" %}

{% trans "Ohm's Law:" %}

V = I × R

{% trans "Where:" %}

  • V = {% trans "Voltage" %} ({% trans "Volts" %})
  • I = {% trans "Current" %} ({% trans "Amperes" %})
  • R = {% trans "Resistance" %} ({% trans "Ohms" %})

{% trans "Power Formulas:" %}

  • P = V × I {% trans "(Power from voltage and current)" %}
  • P = V² / R {% trans "(Power from voltage and resistance)" %}
  • P = I² × R {% trans "(Power from current and resistance)" %}
  • E = P × t {% trans "(Energy from power and time)" %}

{% trans "Ohm's law is a fundamental principle in electrical engineering that describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit. The power formulas allow you to calculate electrical power consumption in various ways depending on the known parameters." %}
