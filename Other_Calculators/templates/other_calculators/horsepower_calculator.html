{% trans "What is Horsepower?" %}

{% trans "Horsepower (HP) is a unit of power that measures the rate at which work is done. It was originally defined by James Watt to compare the output of steam engines with the power of draft horses. One horsepower equals 745.7 watts or 0.7457 kilowatts. Horsepower is commonly used to measure the power output of engines, motors, and other mechanical devices." %}

{% trans "Why 5252?" %}

{% trans "The constant 5252 in the horsepower formula comes from unit conversions. One horsepower is defined as 550 foot-pounds per second. To convert RPM to revolutions per second, we divide by 60. To convert to foot-pounds per minute, we multiply by 60. The calculation: (550 ft-lb/s × 60 s/min) / (2π rad/rev) = 5252. This constant ensures that horsepower and torque curves intersect at 5252 RPM when plotted on the same graph." %}
