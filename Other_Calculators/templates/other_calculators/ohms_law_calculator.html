{% trans "What is Ohm's Law?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}Ohm's law is a simple rule in electronics. It says that the voltage across a conductor is equal to the current flowing through it times the resistance. In short: V = I × R. This tool lets you find any missing value when you know the other two. For resistor-specific calculations, the{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Resistor Calculator" %} {% trans "decodes color codes and combines series/parallel resistors." %}

{% trans "All Formulas at a Glance" %}

{% trans "Voltage" %}: V = I × R

{% trans "Current" %}: I = V / R

{% trans "Resistance" %}: R = V / I

{% trans "Power" %}: P = V × I  |  P = I² × R  |  P = V² / R
