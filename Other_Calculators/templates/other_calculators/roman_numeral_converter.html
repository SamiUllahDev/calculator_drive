    {% trans "What Are Roman Numerals?" %}

    {% blocktrans %}Roman numerals are a numeral system originating in ancient Rome. They use combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet — I, V, X, L, C, D, and M — to represent values. Unlike our modern decimal system, Roman numerals do not use place value in the same way and have no symbol for zero.{% endblocktrans %}

    {% trans "Additive Rule" %}

    {% trans "When a smaller or equal symbol follows a larger one, add the values. Example: VI = 5 + 1 = 6" %}

    {% trans "Subtractive Rule" %}

    {% trans "When a smaller symbol precedes a larger one, subtract the smaller. Example: IV = 5 - 1 = 4. Only I, X, and C can be subtracted." %}

    {% trans "Repetition Rule" %}
