{% trans "What is Weight? Understanding Weight vs Mass" %}

{% blocktrans %}Weight is the gravitational force exerted on an object. When you step on a scale and see kilograms or pounds, you are measuring how strongly gravity pulls on your body. Unlike mass — which is the actual amount of matter and stays the same everywhere — your weight changes depending on your location. For example, you would weigh about one-sixth on the Moon compared to Earth because the Moon's gravity is weaker.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Mass vs Weight: What's the Difference?" %}

{% blocktrans %}Many people use "mass" and "weight" interchangeably, but they are fundamentally different in physics. Understanding this difference is essential for accurate weight conversions and scientific calculations.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Mass (Constant)" %}

  • {% trans "Amount of matter in an object" %}
  • {% trans "Measured in kilograms (kg), grams (g), pounds (lb)" %}
  • {% trans "Stays the same on Earth, Moon, Mars, or in space" %}
  • {% trans "Scalar quantity — has magnitude only" %}

{% trans "Weight (Variable)" %}

  • {% trans "Gravitational force pulling on an object" %}
  • {% trans "Measured in Newtons (N), pound-force (lbf)" %}
  • {% trans "Changes depending on gravity — less on Moon, more on Jupiter" %}
  • {% trans "Vector quantity — has magnitude and direction" %}
